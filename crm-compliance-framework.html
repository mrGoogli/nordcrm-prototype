CRM-COMPLIANCE-DK · v1.0 · April 2026

Compliance Framework
Danish Financial Sector CRM

⚠ High Regulatory Risk
Finanstilsynet Supervised

This document defines all mandatory compliance requirements for a cloud-based CRM operating in the Danish financial sector, covering GDPR, Databeskyttelsesloven, DORA, NIS2, the Danish Financial Business Act, and AML/KYC obligations.

GDPR (EU 2016/679) Databeskyttelsesloven DORA (Jan 2025) NIS2-loven (Jul 2025) Lov om Finansiel Virksomhed AML / KYC
⚠ Important: The Danish financial sector operates under a layered regulatory stack. For financial entities, DORA supersedes NIS2 as lex specialis (from 17 Jan 2025). GDPR is supplemented by Databeskyttelsesloven (Act No. 289, 2024) and sector-specific rules in the Danish Financial Business Act (Act No. 1013, 2024). Non-compliance with DORA carries fines of up to €10M or 5% of global turnover. A qualified legal advisor (DPO + financial compliance counsel) is strongly recommended.
01

Applicable Regulatory Frameworks

GDPR
In Force
EU Regulation 2016/679. Core data protection law. Applies to all processing of personal data of EU residents. In Denmark, supplemented by Databeskyttelsesloven.
Max fine: €20M or 4% global turnover
Databeskyttelsesloven
Act No. 289, 2024
Danish national supplement to GDPR. Adds specific obligations for private-sector data controllers, credit information agencies, and sector-specific processing. Enforced by Datatilsynet.
Supervised by: Datatilsynet (DPA)
DORA
Active Jan 2025
Digital Operational Resilience Act (EU 2022/2554). Mandatory for financial entities and their ICT third-party providers. Supersedes NIS2 for financial sector. Governs ICT risk, incident reporting, testing, and third-party oversight.
Max fine: €10M or 5% global turnover · Supervised by: Finanstilsynet
NIS2-loven
Jul 2025
Implemented in Denmark July 2025. Sector-specific for financial digital infrastructure. For financial entities, DORA takes precedence (lex specialis). NIS2 applies to designated IT suppliers not covered by DORA.
Supervised by: Digitaliseringsstyrelsen + CFCS
Lov om Finansiel Virksomhed
Act No. 1013, 2024
Danish Financial Business Act. Contains sector-specific data protection rules supplementing GDPR. Applies to banks, insurance companies, payment institutions, investment firms. Chapter 19c governs DORA/NIS2 implementation.
Supervised by: Finanstilsynet (FSA)
AML / KYC / PSD2
Required
Anti-money laundering obligations, Know Your Customer requirements, and PSD2 Open Banking rules. Critical for Stacc integration. Forthcoming EU AML package will tighten requirements further.
Supervised by: Finanstilsynet + SØIK
02

GDPR & Databeskyttelsesloven Requirements

Requirement CRM Implementation Priority
Lawful Basis per Article 6 Every data processing activity in the CRM must have a documented lawful basis (consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interest). Financial sector often relies on Article 6(1)(b) contract and 6(1)(c) legal obligation for client data. Critical
Data Processing Register (RoPA) Maintain a full Record of Processing Activities per Article 30 in the CRM system itself — every data category, processing purpose, retention period, and third-party processor listed per module (Sales, Support, Marketing). Critical
Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) Mandatory DPIA required before launch — Article 35 GDPR + Datatilsynet guidance. Required because the CRM processes financial personal data at scale, uses AI/profiling (lead scoring), and integrates Stacc open banking data. Datatilsynet must be consulted if high risk remains after DPIA. Critical
Data Minimisation (Art. 5) Collect only what is necessary per module. Marketing module must not store full financial records. Define field-level access controls so each module only accesses the data it needs. AI models must not train on more data than required. High
Retention & Deletion Automated data retention policies: financial client data typically 5–10 years under Danish bookkeeping law (Bogføringsloven). CRM must enforce configurable retention schedules per data type with automatic anonymisation or deletion workflows. Critical
Data Subject Rights Build workflows for: Right of Access (Art. 15), Right to Erasure (Art. 17), Right to Rectification (Art. 16), Right to Restriction (Art. 18), Right to Portability (Art. 20). Erasure must cascade across Cosmos DB, Blob Storage, search index, and backups. Critical
Consent Management Marketing module requires granular opt-in consent (not pre-ticked). Consent records must be stored with timestamp, version, and withdrawal mechanism. Withdrawals must automatically suppress contact from all campaigns. High
Data Processor Agreements (DPA) Written DPA required with every sub-processor: Microsoft Azure (available via Microsoft's DPA), Stacc, any AI provider (Azure OpenAI). Danish Datatilsynet standard clauses should be used or referenced. DPA terms must include Art. 28(3) requirements. Critical
International Data Transfers All data must remain in EU/EEA Azure regions (North Europe / West Europe). Any transfer to US services (e.g. Azure OpenAI US endpoints) must be under EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision or SCCs. Post-Schrems II: verify per vendor. Critical
Privacy by Design & Default All new CRM features must undergo privacy review before release. Default settings must be the most privacy-protective option. Documented in the SDLC (Software Development Lifecycle) policy. High
AI & Profiling (Art. 22) Lead scoring and credit risk profiling using AI requires: explicit disclosure to data subjects, human oversight option, and right to contest automated decisions. Datatilsynet is actively focused on AI/profiling enforcement in 2025–2026. Critical
Breach Notification (72h) Must notify Datatilsynet within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach (Art. 33). High-risk breaches require notification to affected individuals. Build automated breach detection and reporting workflows in Azure Sentinel. Critical
03

DORA — Digital Operational Resilience Requirements

DORA Pillar CRM Architecture Requirement Priority
ICT Risk Management Framework Formal ICT risk policy documented at board level. CRM must have a risk register covering: data loss, system outages, third-party failures (Stacc, M365), cyber incidents. Annual review required. Azure's built-in risk tools + custom register in CRM. Critical
Incident Classification & Reporting Major ICT incidents must be reported to Finanstilsynet: Initial notification within 4 hours (max 24h from awareness), intermediate report within 72 hours, final report within 1 month. Build automated incident classification using Azure Monitor + SIEM. Must distinguish major vs. significant incidents per DORA RTS criteria. Critical
Digital Operational Resilience Testing Annual basic digital resilience testing required. For significant financial institutions: Threat-Led Penetration Testing (TLPT) every 3 years by certified external testers. CRM must be included in the test scope. Azure's penetration testing policy must be followed for cloud components. High
Third-Party ICT Risk (Art. 28–44) Written ICT contracts with Microsoft Azure and Stacc must include DORA Art. 30 mandatory clauses: exit strategies, audit rights, SLAs, data location, incident notification obligations. Maintain a register of all ICT third-party dependencies. Finanstilsynet may audit ICT providers. Critical
Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery Documented BCP and DR plan for the CRM. Define RPO (Recovery Point Objective) and RTO (Recovery Time Objective). Azure geo-redundant setup: primary North Europe, secondary West Europe. Regular DR testing required (at least annually). Finanstilsynet expects board-approved BCP. High
ICT Audit (Internal) ICT risk management framework subject to annual internal audit. Formal follow-up process on critical findings. Audit trail for all privileged access to CRM data and infrastructure stored in Azure Monitor (min 1 year retention). High
Supply Chain / Concentration Risk Avoid over-dependence on single ICT provider. Assess concentration risk if both M365 and Azure are from Microsoft. Document exit strategy from key providers. Finanstilsynet and EBA are focused on cloud concentration risk in 2025–2026. Medium
04

Personal Data Flows & Residency

📥 Data In — Sources
  • Customer contact data entered by CRM users (Sales, Support, Marketing modules)
  • M365 Graph API: email metadata, calendar events, Teams meeting records
  • Stacc Open Banking: financial transactions, account data, credit scores (PSD2 / GDPR Art. 9 if sensitive)
  • Web forms / lead capture (must include GDPR consent notice)
  • Imported datasets (CSV/Excel) — must validate lawful basis on import
  • Azure AD / Entra ID: employee user data (separate controller relationship)
📤 Data Out — Destinations
  • Azure Cosmos DB: primary storage — EU North Europe region only
  • Azure Blob Storage: documents, attachments — EU region only
  • Azure AI Search: indexed contact/deal data — EU region
  • Power BI Embedded: analytics — EU region, no raw PII in reports
  • Azure OpenAI: AI features — must use EU-deployed endpoint, no data training retention
  • SharePoint/OneDrive: documents shared per deal — EU tenant required
  • Datatilsynet: breach notifications within 72h (mandatory)
  • Finanstilsynet: DORA major incident reports (mandatory)
🏦 Stacc Financial Data — Special Rules
  • Financial data from Stacc is processed under PSD2 and must only be accessed with explicit customer consent
  • Must be stored in field-encrypted Cosmos DB containers, isolated per tenant
  • Credit and risk data may constitute sensitive data requiring heightened Art. 9 justification
  • Retention limited to the purpose — cannot be used for marketing without separate consent
  • Stacc Data Processing Agreement must include DORA Art. 30 ICT clauses
  • KYC/AML data subject to separate retention rules (typically 5 years minimum under Danish AML Act)
📍 Data Residency Rules
  • Primary: Azure North Europe (Ireland/Denmark datacenter region)
  • DR/Backup: Azure West Europe — no data leaves EU/EEA
  • M365 tenant must be set to EU data boundary (Microsoft EU Data Boundary programme)
  • Azure OpenAI: use Sweden Central or West Europe deployments only
  • No personal data may transit to US endpoints without SCCs or Data Privacy Framework compliance
  • Regular data flow mapping (Art. 30 RoPA) must be updated when new integrations are added

Compliance Implementation Checklist

Legal & GovernanceBefore development starts
Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO)Mandatory for financial sector entities processing personal data at scale. Register with Datatilsynet.
Conduct full DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)Required before launch. Consult Datatilsynet if residual high risk. Document all risks and mitigations.
Establish Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)Art. 30 GDPR. Document every processing activity, data category, purpose, lawful basis, retention, and processors.
Sign Data Processor Agreements (DPA)With Microsoft Azure, Stacc, and any sub-processors. Use Datatilsynet standard clauses. Include DORA Art. 30 ICT clauses for ICT providers.
Develop ICT Risk Management Framework (DORA)Board-approved policy. ICT risk register, annual review cycle. Required by Finanstilsynet.
Register ICT third-party providers (DORA)Maintain register of all ICT vendors (Microsoft, Stacc). Include contractual DORA terms in all ICT agreements.
Technical ArchitecturePhase 1 — Foundation
Deploy all infrastructure in EU Azure regions onlyNorth Europe + West Europe. Enforce via Azure Policy. Enable M365 EU Data Boundary.
Implement field-level encryption for financial dataStacc data and credit records encrypted at field level in Cosmos DB. Keys in Azure Key Vault, customer-managed keys (CMK).
Build data retention engineConfigurable retention schedules per data type. Automated deletion / anonymisation workflows. Bogføringsloven compliance (5-year minimum for financial records).
Implement Data Subject Rights portalSelf-service or admin-managed workflows for access, erasure, rectification, portability requests. Cascading deletion across all data stores including backups.
Consent management system (Marketing module)Granular opt-in, timestamp + version stored, withdrawal mechanism, automatic suppression from all campaigns on withdrawal.
Comprehensive audit loggingAll data access, modifications, deletions, and exports logged. Minimum 1-year retention in Azure Monitor. Immutable log storage. Required for DORA ICT audit trail.
Multi-tenant data isolationStrict tenant isolation at Cosmos DB container level. No cross-tenant data access possible at infrastructure level.
Security & DORA ResiliencePhase 1–2
Azure Sentinel SIEM + 72h breach detectionAutomated breach detection, classification, and Datatilsynet notification workflow. DORA incident classification per RTS criteria.
Disaster Recovery plan with defined RPO/RTODocument and test annually. Geo-redundant Cosmos DB failover. Board-approved. Required for DORA Art. 11.
DORA incident reporting pipeline to FinanstilsynetAutomated workflow: initial (4h), intermediate (72h), final (1 month) reports. Integrate with Finanstilsynet's DORA reporting portal.
Annual penetration testingBasic annual tests. TLPT (Threat-Led Penetration Testing) every 3 years for significant entities. Required by DORA. Azure penetration testing policy must be followed.
Conditional Access & Zero TrustEntra ID Conditional Access policies. MFA mandatory. Privileged Identity Management (PIM) for admin roles. Least-privilege RBAC across all CRM modules.
AI Features (Datatilsynet Focus Area 2025–26)Phase 3–4
Disclose AI/profiling use to data subjectsArt. 13/14 GDPR. Inform customers when their data is used for lead scoring, credit risk profiling, or automated decisions.
Human review option for AI decisions (Art. 22)Any automated decision with significant effect on a person must offer human review on request. Implement in lead scoring and credit risk modules.
Azure OpenAI data processing termsEnsure Azure OpenAI is configured with no-training-on-prompts setting. Use EU endpoint only. Include in DPA.
EU AI Act compliance assessmentEU AI Act in force Aug 2024, progressively applicable. Assess if AI features qualify as high-risk AI systems (credit scoring likely does). Plan accordingly.

Data Breach & ICT Incident Response Timeline

T+0 — Discovery
Incident Detected & Classified
Azure Sentinel or manual detection. Security team assesses: is this a personal data breach (GDPR) and/or a major ICT incident (DORA)? Classification documented in incident register. Incident Commander assigned.
T+4 hours (DORA) — No later than T+24h
Initial DORA Notification → Finanstilsynet
If classified as major ICT incident under DORA: notify Finanstilsynet via their reporting portal within 4 hours of classification (max 24h from awareness). Notify Stacc and Microsoft via contractual channels.
T+72 hours (GDPR + DORA)
Datatilsynet Notification (GDPR) + DORA Intermediate Report
If personal data breach: notify Datatilsynet within 72h (Art. 33 GDPR) unless breach unlikely to present risk. Simultaneously submit DORA intermediate report to Finanstilsynet with updated scope, impact, and containment measures.
T+72h (if high risk) — GDPR Art. 34
Individual Notification
If breach likely results in high risk to data subjects (e.g. financial data exposed): notify affected individuals without undue delay. Include what happened, what data, what they should do. No fixed deadline but must be prompt.
T+1 month (DORA)
DORA Final Report → Finanstilsynet
Submit final incident report within one month of incident resolution. Include root cause analysis, full impact assessment, remediation actions taken, and future prevention measures. Internal post-mortem also required.

Roles, Responsibilities & Authorities

Required Roles
  • DPO (Databeskyttelsesrådgiver) — Mandatory. Register with Datatilsynet. Independent, expert in data protection law.
  • CISO / ICT Risk Manager — Owns DORA compliance. Reports to board. Manages ICT risk register and incident response.
  • Compliance Officer — Financial Business Act & AML compliance. Coordinates with Finanstilsynet.
  • Data Processor Manager — Manages third-party DPAs (Microsoft, Stacc). Monitors sub-processor compliance.
  • Incident Commander — On-call role. Coordinates breach response across GDPR + DORA timelines.
Regulatory Authorities
  • Datatilsynet — Danish Data Protection Agency. GDPR + Databeskyttelsesloven enforcement. datatilsynet.dk
  • Finanstilsynet — Danish FSA. DORA + Financial Business Act enforcement. finanstilsynet.dk
  • CFCS — Centre for Cyber Security. NIS2 national hub. cfcs.dk
  • Digitaliseringsstyrelsen — NIS2 oversight for non-financial entities. digst.dk
  • SØIK — State Attorney for Serious Economic Crime. AML enforcement.
  • EDPB — European Data Protection Board. Cross-border GDPR guidance.
